# AI-MCN Presentation Script (Research-Expanded)

## Core 15-Minute Path (Use Slides 1-15)

## 0:00-1:00 | Speaker A | Title and Team (Slides 1-2)
- Introduce project, team, and one-line value proposition.
- Clarify this deck has backup research slides after core section.

## 1:00-3:30 | Speaker A | Business Context and Problem (Slides 3-6)
- Use market growth numbers (EMARKETER, IAB) to show stakes.
- Explain operational pain: creator selection friction, manual workload, ROI proof issues.
- Explain MCN role and gap: useful intermediaries, but opaque matching logic for brands.

## 3:30-5:30 | Speaker A | Why Beauty + Why BOJ (Slides 7-8)
- Explain beauty complexity: social influence still high, CAC pressure, fast trend turnover.
- Explain BOJ fit: clear product narrative, U.S. activation story, good case specificity.

## 5:30-7:30 | Speaker B | Data and Features (Slides 9-11)
- Data scale and source.
- Preprocessing and quality controls.
- Feature architecture: network/text/performance/ML/reliability.

## 7:30-10:30 | Speaker B | AI/ML Approach (Slides 12-15)
- SNA graph + communities.
- TF-IDF and semantic/tone.
- ML suite with GroupKFold(5), baseline comparison, SHAP.
- Final ranking formula + credibility multiplier + diversity guardrail.

## 10:30-13:30 | Speaker C | Prototype Demo (Slides 16-20)
- Show best-model result.
- Show top-match card anatomy and rationale.
- Show Network/Text/ML/ROI tabs quickly with one insight each.

## 13:30-14:30 | Speaker C | Impact, Limitations, Next Steps (Slides 21-23)
- Impact: speed + transparency + reduced mismatch risk.
- Limitations and governance.
- 90-day roadmap to pilot.

## 14:30-15:00 | Speaker C (or All) | AI Tools + Q&A (Slides 24-25)
- How AI tools helped vs what remained human-owned.
- Close and open Q&A.
